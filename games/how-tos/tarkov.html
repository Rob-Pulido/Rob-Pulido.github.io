Escape From Tarkov

How To Play

Starting out

In Escape From Tarkov, there is never a dull moment. Be prepared to think fast and move fast! Unless joining with a team, everyone is your enemy. You're first priority should be to gather loot and survive the round by extracting in a designated area on the map. Get to know the maps! Unlike most games, the HUD for Tarkov is extremely minimal, so knowing where you are through identification of your surroundings is a must. See someone? Shoot them! Because without a doubt, whether player or Scav (Player/AI), they will shoot to kill.

Whats Next

Play a few rounds, get to know the gameplay and mechanics, then you'll realize you've probably lost more than you've gained at this point.. Luckily, you dont have to risk everything you come in with every round. In Tarkov, you have the option to play as a player scav every 20 minutes. Which means, you go in-place of AI, and the game will equip you (minimally) with enough gear to survive. If you do survive, you keep everything you gained (including what you started with) from the round. This can be extremly profitable, if you know the best routes to loot, and are lucky enough to survive the round without running into extremely geared players. Although, catching a geared player offguard happens quite often. Additionally, a faceshot is a faceshot. So if you're talented and/or lucky, you could extract pretty wealthy.

End Game

There really is no end game when it comes to Escape From Tarkov. Eventually, your account may become stacked with items. You may get to a point where you enter rounds more geared than you ever thought possible. It really doesnt negate the fact that pvp is the sole basis for this game. While being heavily geared in armor, and having highly penetrable bullets, if you are trash with your aim, you wont stand a chance. So practice, practice, practice!
